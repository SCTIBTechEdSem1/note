Syllabus

Unit One:
Advanced Grammar (10 hrs.)
1. Mixed Tenses; Active and Passive Voice; Reported Speech; Subject-Verb Agreement; Relative Clauses; Connectives/Conjunctions; Conditionals; Inversion and Cleft Sentences; Comparisons; Substitution and Elimination; Prepositions; Articles

Unit Two:
Technical Communication (8 hrs.)
1. Basics of communication; Communication process; Overcoming communication barriers; Audience recognition
2. Objectives: Clarity; Conciseness; Accuracy; Organization; Ethics
3. Writing process: Prewriting; Writing; Rewriting
4. Basic digital skills: Document design; Web 2.0 skills

Unit Three:
Oral skills (8 hrs.)
1. Technical presentations: the process, delivery, and update
2. Preparing visuals; Seminars: (paper-based) and virtual
3. Workshops and panel discussions; Negotiation skills for professionals; Idea pitching; Classroom communication

Unit Four:
Organizational /Business Writing (10 hrs.)
1. Memos; Letters: job application, cover, inquiry, recommendations/references; Emails; Curriculum vitae and Resumes; Team communication: meeting and minutes
2. Proposals: concept papers, research and project proposals; White papers; conference papers, technical articles and project reports

Unit Five:
Rhetorical strategies (definition, purpose, audience and strategies) (12 hrs.)
1. Description: Objective: spatial/physical, graphic; Subjective - Key Reading: Virginia Woolf: "The Death of the Moth"
2. Narration: chronology; reverse chronology; flashback - Key Reading: Nicholas Gage: "The Teacher Who Changed My Life"
3. Process Analysis: How equipment/systems work; How humans perform tasks - Key Reading: Armand Dennis: "The Four-Tusked Elephant"
4. Comparison and Contrast: point-by-point method; block method - Key Reading: Robert Jastrow: "Brains and Computers"
5. Cause and Effect: proximate and distant cause/effect - Key Reading: Norman Cousins: "Pain Is Not the Ultimate Enemy"
6. Persuasion and Argument: AIDA Model (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action); Toulmin's Model (claim, ground, warrant, backing, rebuttal, modifier) - Key Reading: H. L. Mencken: "The Penalty of Death"

Unit Five:
